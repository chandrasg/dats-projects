# Get Started

Follow these steps to enroll in DATS 5990 (Practicum) or DATS 9990 (Thesis).

## Before You Begin

<div class="info-box">
Students typically pursue a thesis or practicum in their 2nd year (2nd or 3rd semester). You should complete at least one elective course in your project's application domain before starting.
</div>

## Enrollment Steps

<ol class="steps">
<li>
<strong>Identify a faculty advisor</strong><br>
Find a Penn faculty member willing to supervise your project. Two approaches that work well: (1) approach a faculty member whose course you took and who has expertise aligned with your interests, or (2) search "upenn research &lt;your domain&gt;" and email faculty who work in your area of interest. See the <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1iIi1KfWK8GDqGP3Bpz3bU5oN1zaEhs4IBiDr_JG84G4/edit" target="_blank">DATS Focus Areas Handbook</a> for guidance on campus research groups.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Define your project scope</strong><br>
Discuss the topic and scope with your advisor. Even if details aren't fully worked out, identify the broad domain, the research questions you'll explore, and your specific role in the project. For a thesis, this should be a project you lead end-to-end. For a practicum, your contribution can be a distinct piece of a larger effort.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Prepare your proposal</strong><br>
Write a 2-page project proposal covering the project scope, your role, and how you'll contribute. This doesn't need to be final — it's a planning tool to define the broad direction with your advisor.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Get the advisor agreement signed</strong><br>
Download the <a href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/19d3zkQQ5TE014Onx_T0yOSQOgVGeGfKO/view" target="_blank">Advisor Agreement Form</a>, have your faculty advisor sign it, and keep the signed copy ready for upload.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Submit the project form</strong><br>
Fill out the <a href="https://forms.gle/fYeA2nXtkgzqLpio6" target="_blank">Project/Thesis Submission Form</a>. You'll upload your signed advisor agreement and project proposal as part of this form.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Request the course in Path@Penn</strong><br>
Request either <strong>DATS 5990</strong> (Practicum) or <strong>DATS 9990</strong> (Thesis) in Path@Penn. Once approved, Staci Kaplan (Program Manager) will issue your permit.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Present your proposal to the Project Director</strong><br>
Present your proposal to <a href="mailto:{{ site.data.contacts.project_director.email }}">{{ site.data.contacts.project_director.name }}</a> (in-person or via email) so the Project Director is aware of your project scope.
</li>
</ol>

<div class="info-box warning">
<strong>Timing matters.</strong> Aim to complete your submission form and advisor agreement <strong>before</strong> the add/drop deadline. Check the <a href="{{ '/timeline/' | relative_url }}">Timeline</a> for current semester dates.
</div>

## Choosing Between Thesis and Practicum

Not sure which is right for you? Here's how to think about it:

**Choose Thesis (DATS 9990) if** you want to lead an entire research project from start to finish, your work could result in a peer-reviewed publication, and you're ready for a two-semester commitment with a SEAS Standing Faculty advisor.

**Choose Practicum (DATS 5990) if** you want to contribute meaningfully to a research project (potentially as part of a larger team), you prefer a shorter engagement (one semester, one CU), or your advisor is outside SEAS. Industry-based projects are also an option here, as long as they produce an academically evaluable product.
