# Frequently Asked Questions

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can I take a 2 CU Practicum in a single semester?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Typically, a Practicum is a 1 CU course in a single semester. In some cases, 2 CUs in the same semester are allowed based on your advisor's and Project Director's discretion and the project scope.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can I take 2 CU Thesis in a single semester?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
No. You should take 1 CU of Thesis in two consecutive semesters.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>When is the earliest I can start a Thesis or Practicum?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Students typically do a thesis or practicum in their 2nd year (2nd or 3rd semester).
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>If I'm working on a practicum or thesis across two semesters, do I get a letter grade for both semesters?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Yes. You receive a letter grade each semester and need to submit deliverables — a final report and a presentation or poster — at the end of each semester. Work with your faculty advisor to clearly define deliverables for each semester. At the end of the semester, your advisor will be consulted on their assessment of your progress and a grade will be assigned accordingly.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>I'm interested in enrolling but not sure which faculty to reach out to. How do I find an advisor?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Two strategies that have worked well: (1) approach a faculty member whose course you took and who has expertise aligned with your interests, and (2) search "upenn research &lt;your domain&gt;" and email the faculty members who work in the area you're interested in to explore if they'd be willing to serve as your advisor. The <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1iIi1KfWK8GDqGP3Bpz3bU5oN1zaEhs4IBiDr_JG84G4/edit" target="_blank">DATS Focus Areas Handbook</a> is also a great resource for finding campus research groups.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>My advisor is busy and we can't hash out all the project details before the deadline. What should I include in the proposal?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
If you've identified a faculty member who will sign the advisor agreement form, use that time to briefly discuss the project domain and some research questions you'd likely explore. The 2-page project proposal is a tool for you to identify the broad scope of the project, your role in it, and how you would contribute. It doesn't need to be final.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can I work in a team towards a Practicum project?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Yes. You can work as a team of 2–3 students if your faculty advisor signs off on it and each team member has a clearly distinct set of deliverables. This should be identified upon discussion with your faculty advisor and mentioned in the advisor agreement form.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Can I get paid for a project I'm enrolling for Practicum/Thesis credit?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
No. You cannot receive payment for a project you are also enrolling for academic credit.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What is the format of the project report?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
<p><strong>Practicum:</strong> A 5–10 page report describing the project, background, data used, methods developed/employed, and takeaways/conclusions. Include links to any evaluable code.</p>
<p><strong>Thesis:</strong> Follow <a href="https://grad.seas.upenn.edu/student-handbook/masters-thesis/" target="_blank">SEAS thesis formatting guidelines</a>. For citations, follow the Chicago Manual of Style, MLA Handbook, or the appropriate manual for your field. See the <a href="https://grad.seas.upenn.edu/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/MS-sample-thesis-title-picture.pdf" target="_blank">sample title page</a>. You'll need three signatures on the title page: your advisor, Dr. Guntuku (Project Director), and Prof. Sue Davidson (Program Director).</p>
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What are the presentation guidelines?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
<p><strong>Thesis:</strong> Prepare a 5-minute presentation for DATS Presentation Day.</p>
<p><strong>Practicum:</strong> Present a poster (24 × 36 inches, landscape orientation). Poster printing is available through <a href="https://cets.seas.upenn.edu/answers/posters.html" target="_blank">CETS</a>. Contact the Program Manager for the budget code. Easels and poster board will be provided.</p>
<p>All students upload their presentations/posters to the shared Google Drive folder before the event. Check the <a href="{{ '/timeline/' | relative_url }}">Timeline</a> for specific deadlines.</p>
</div>
</details>
